Canada Place & Coal Harbour Walk
Afternoon
✨ Fun FactCanada Place's iconic white sails were designed to look like a giant ship! Built for Expo 86, it's now a cruise ship terminal — you might see a massive ship docked right there.

Afternoon walk along the waterfront with the North Shore mountains straight ahead. Coal Harbour is home to some of the most expensive real estate in Canada — spot the float planes taking off from the harbour!

Gastown Orientation Walk
Late Afternoon
✨ Fun FactGastown is named after "Gassy Jack" Deighton — a saloon owner famous for talking nonstop! It's Vancouver's oldest neighbourhood, founded in 1867, before the city even existed.

The famous Steam Clock at Water & Cambie whistles every 15 minutes — powered by an underground steam system that still heats buildings beneath the streets. See Maple Tree Square and historic Water Street.

🔗 Gastown guide

Vancouver Art Gallery
Morning
✨ Fun FactThe Vancouver Art Gallery is housed in a former courthouse built in 1906! It has the largest collection of works by Emily Carr — one of Canada's most famous artists, known for her powerful paintings of BC forests and Indigenous villages.

One of Canada's top art galleries, right in the heart of downtown. The building itself is a stunning neoclassical courthouse. Rated 4.3/5 by thousands of visitors.

Lunch & Robson Street Shopping

Robson Street is Vancouver's main shopping corridor — bookstores, clothing boutiques, Pacific Centre mall, and tons of lunch spots. Casual browsing and coffee after the gallery.

🔗 Robson Street guide

Dinner at Nightingale
Dinner

One of Vancouver's most celebrated restaurants by chef David Hawksworth. Shared plates, incredible pasta, fresh seafood. Rated 4.5/5 and a favourite for special occasions.

Easter Sunday & Stanley Park 🌸

Sunday, April 5  ·  Brunch at Home · Seawall · Totem Poles · Aquarium · Soup Dumplings

⏳Loading weather…
🥚
Easter Brunch at Home
Morning

Happy Easter! Start the day right with a homemade brunch — whatever you picked out last night. Cook together, eat well, and ease into the morning before heading out to the park. 🥞🍳🥐

🚌
Public Transit to Stanley Park
Getting There

Hop on the bus from downtown — transit drops you right at the park entrance. Easy, no parking stress, and one less thing to think about on a holiday Sunday.

🌲
Explore Stanley Park
Morning / Afternoon
✨ Fun FactStanley Park is one of the largest urban parks in North America at 400 hectares — almost as big as downtown Vancouver itself! The park opened in 1888, and some of its Douglas fir trees are over 500 years old.
  • ☀️ Sunny day? Rent bikes and ride the famous seawall loop — 8.8km of ocean and mountain views the whole way.
  • 🌧️ Rainy day? Rent a car from Turo and drive Stanley Park Drive instead.

Either way, hit the highlights: the Totem Poles, Brockton Point Lighthouse, the Nine O'Clock Gun, Ferguson Point, Lumberman's Arch, and the ancient Hollow Trees.

🔗 Stanley Park info
🍺
Stanley Park Brewing — Apps & Refreshments
12:30 Reservation

Reservation at 12:30 for apps and drinks — a well-earned break right in the park. Local craft beers, casual bites, and a gorgeous setting among the trees.

🐋
Vancouver Aquarium
Afternoon
✨ Fun FactThe Vancouver Aquarium was the first in Canada to train dolphins! Today it focuses on rescue and rehabilitation — many of the animals there were rescued from the wild and cannot be released back into the ocean.

One of Canada's largest aquariums, right inside Stanley Park. Sea otters, jellyfish, sharks, a 4D experience, and touch tanks. Rated 4.5/5 by over 11,000 visitors. Buy tickets online in advance!

🔗 Aquarium website
🚲
Return Bikes & Wander the West End
Late Afternoon

Drop the bikes off and drift through the West End — one of Vancouver's most charming and walkable neighbourhoods. Stroll Denman and Davie, browse shops, and work up an appetite for the feast ahead.

🔗 West End guide
🥟
Easter Feast at Din Tai Fung
Dinner · 6:30 Reservation
✨ The DTF StoryDin Tai Fung started in 1958 as a humble cooking oil shop in Taipei, Taiwan, founded by Bing-Yi Yang — who arrived in Taiwan from China at age 20 with just $20 in his pocket. When oil sales declined, the Yangs dedicated half the shop to handmaking Xiao Long Bao in 1972. The dumplings became so beloved they dropped the oil business entirely. By 1993, the New York Times named DTF one of the top ten gourmet restaurants in the world. Today it spans 165+ locations across 13 countries, and has been awarded a Michelin Star five times at its Hong Kong branch. Still family-owned, still made by hand.

One of our all-time family favourites — and the perfect way to celebrate Easter! Reservation at 6:30. The spread: soup dumplings, xiao long bao, potstickers, noodles, minced pork noodles, green beans, pork buns — and one each of the dessert buns to finish. A feast for the ages. 🥢

🔗 DTF Menu📖 Our Story
🛋️
Lyft Home for Couch Time
Evening

Call a Lyft, head home, and collapse on the couch. A full, wonderful Easter Sunday. Rest up — there's more adventure ahead.

North Vancouver & Game Night 🌉

Monday, April 6  ·  Seabus · Lonsdale Quay · Capilano Bridge · Game Night at home

⏳Loading weather…
⛴️
Seabus to Lonsdale Quay Market
Morning
✨ Fun FactThe Seabus crosses Burrard Inlet in just 12 minutes and is one of the busiest water crossings in North America. It runs on regular public transit — just tap your card to ride!

Take the Seabus from Waterfront Station across to North Vancouver. Lonsdale Quay is a fantastic public market with fresh food, artisan shops, and incredible views of Vancouver's skyline from across the water. Pick up tonight's charcuterie supplies here!

🔗 Lonsdale Quay
🌉
Capilano Suspension Bridge Park
Late Morning / Afternoon
✨ Fun FactThe Capilano Suspension Bridge is 137 metres long and hangs 70 metres above the Capilano River — as tall as a 23-storey building! The original bridge was built in 1889 using hemp rope and cedar planks.

Walk across the swaying suspension bridge, then explore the Treetops Adventure (walkways high between giant Douglas firs) and the thrilling Cliffwalk along the canyon face. Rated 4.6/5 with nearly 37,000 reviews!

🔗 Capilano Bridge website
🧀
Charcuterie, Wine & Game Night at the Airbnb
Evening
🌙 Tonight's VibeAfter a big adventure day on the North Shore, the perfect evening is to slow right down. Grab charcuterie, cheese, bread, and snacks from Lonsdale Quay on the way back — plus wine and juice — for a relaxed game night.

Lay out the snacks, grab your favourite games, and settle in. Good food, good company, no agenda. Sometimes the best travel memories happen around a table at home.

Granville Island & Science World 🔬

Tuesday, April 7  ·  Aquabus · Public Market · False Creek Walk · Science World

⏳Loading weather…
🛶
Aquabus to Granville Island
Morning
✨ Fun FactGranville Island is actually a peninsula, not a real island! It used to be an industrial area of factories and warehouses. In the 1970s, the government converted it into an arts and culture hub — today there are over 300 studios, theatres, and shops.

Take the tiny Aquabus ferry across False Creek for a fun 5-minute boat ride! The Public Market is incredible for breakfast — fresh pastries, cheeses, smoked salmon, and local produce. Explore the Net Loft artisan shops and browse artisan studios.

🔗 Granville Island🔗 Aquabus ferries
🥐
Lunch — Graze Through the Market
Lunch

Granville Island Public Market is one of the best places in Vancouver to graze your way through lunch. Pick up fresh bread, artisan cheese, smoked salmon, crepes, freshly squeezed juice, donuts from Lee's — and just wander and snack. Don't miss Lee's Donuts at the far end of the market!

🔗 Lee's Donuts
🚶
False Creek Walk to Olympic Village
Afternoon
✨ Fun FactOlympic Village was built to house 2,800 athletes for the 2010 Winter Olympics. After the Games ended, it was converted into one of Vancouver's most beautiful and sustainable residential neighbourhoods!

Walk the scenic seawall path from Granville Island along False Creek toward Olympic Village, passing public art installations and great waterfront views. A really pretty afternoon stroll.

🔗 Olympic Village guide
🔭
Science World
Afternoon
✨ Fun FactThe giant geodesic dome was built for Expo 86. It's made of 766 triangular panels and stands 47 metres tall — it looks like a giant golf ball! At night it lights up in brilliant colours and is one of Vancouver's most recognizable landmarks.

Expect to spend 3–4 hours here easily. Bubble shows, a massive LEGO area, optical illusions, a live science theatre, and exhibits on everything from the human body to the natural world. Rated 4.5/5 by over 15,000 visitors.

🔗 Science World website
🍜
Dinner in Olympic Village or Yaletown
Evening

Olympic Village has excellent restaurants right on the water. Or head to Yaletown — a converted warehouse district with trendy restaurants, patios, and great energy. End the evening with a walk along the False Creek seawall as the Science World dome lights up.

Chinatown & Strathcona 🏮

Wednesday, April 8  ·  Chinese Canadian Museum · Chinese Garden · Strathcona · Yaletown dinner

⏳Loading weather…
🏛️
Chinese Canadian Museum
Morning
✨ Fun FactThe Chinese Canadian Museum opened in 2023 inside the Wing Sang Building — the oldest building in Vancouver's Chinatown, built in 1889! It's the first museum in Canada dedicated entirely to Chinese Canadian history, and won Canada's top history museum award just two years after opening.

Four floors of exhibits covering Chinese Canadian history from 1788 to today. Personal stories of the gold rush, the railway, the Head Tax, WWII service, and the vibrant community that persevered through it all. Rated 4.8/5.

🔗 Museum website
🌿
Dr. Sun Yat-Sen Classical Chinese Garden
Late Morning
✨ Fun FactThis is the first full-scale classical Chinese garden ever built outside of China! Over 50 artisans came all the way from Suzhou, China to build it in 1986, and every single rock, plant, and tile was imported from China.

A peaceful oasis in the heart of Chinatown. The garden reflects Taoist philosophy — every element has its opposite. Look for koi fish in the jade-green pond. Guided tours are included with admission and are absolutely worth it.

🔗 Garden website
🥟
Lunch at Bao Bei or New Town Bakery
Lunch

Bao Bei is a beloved Chinatown restaurant with creative Chinese-inspired small plates in a beautiful heritage building. New Town Bakery is a Chinatown institution — famous for BBQ pork buns and red bean pastries at incredibly affordable prices.

🏘️
Strathcona Neighbourhood Walk
Afternoon
✨ Fun FactStrathcona is Vancouver's oldest residential neighbourhood, dating back to the 1890s. Many of the original Victorian and Edwardian homes are still standing — some are over 130 years old!

A wander through Strathcona's tree-lined streets, heritage homes, murals, community gardens, and independent coffee shops. A very different and authentic side of Vancouver.

🔗 Strathcona neighbourhood guide
🍷
Dinner in Yaletown
Dinner

Yaletown — a converted warehouse district turned upscale dining destination — has some of Vancouver's best restaurants. Great patios, lively atmosphere, and restaurants to suit any mood.

UBC & Kitsilano 🌿

Thursday, April 9  ·  Museum of Anthropology · Campus Walk · Kits Beach · West 4th

⏳Loading weather…
🚌
Morning Bus to UBC
Morning

Take the bus out to UBC — the University of British Columbia — on a scenic peninsula surrounded by ocean and forest. The bus ride itself is beautiful as you head west through the city and into the forested endowment lands.

🏺
Museum of Anthropology at UBC
Morning
✨ Fun FactThe Museum of Anthropology has one of the world's finest collections of Northwest Coast First Nations art, including some of the tallest totem poles you'll ever see indoors! Designed by Canadian architect Arthur Erickson, it sits on the traditional territory of the Musqueam people.

One of Canada's most celebrated museums, set on the cliffs overlooking the Pacific. Incredible First Nations totem poles, masks, and carvings, plus collections from cultures worldwide. Rated 4.7/5.

🔗 Museum website
🎓
Walk the UBC Campus
Afternoon

UBC's campus is stunning — rose gardens, ocean cliffs, striking architecture, and peaceful forest trails. Worth a wander after the museum before heading to Kitsilano.

🗺️ Self-guided walking tour
🏖️
Kitsilano Beach & West 4th Avenue
Afternoon
✨ Fun FactKitsilano Beach is home to the longest outdoor pool in Canada at 137 metres! On a clear day you can see downtown Vancouver, the mountains, and all the way to Vancouver Island from the beach.

One of Vancouver's most popular beaches with stunning views of downtown and the mountains. West 4th Avenue has indie bookshops, cafes, outdoor gear stores, and local boutiques — great for lunch or a coffee before heading back.

🔗 Kitsilano Beach info

False Creek Ferries, Museums & FlyOver 🌊

Friday, April 10  ·  Maxine's Brunch · False Creek Ferries · Museum of Vancouver · Granville Island · FlyOver Canada

⏳Loading weather…
🥞
Brunch at Maxine's
Morning

Start the day at Maxine's in Yaletown — a cozy neighbourhood favourite for brunch. Great food, relaxed vibe, and a perfect warm-up before a big day on the water and around the waterfront.

🔗 Maxine's on Yelp
⛴️
False Creek Ferries — Day Pass
All Day
✨ Fun FactThe False Creek Ferries have been running since 1983, operating small passenger ferries across False Creek between Granville Island, the West End, Yaletown, Science World, and the Maritime Museum dock. It's one of the most charming and practical ways to see the city — locals use it like a water bus!

Walk to the False Creek Ferry terminal at Sunset Beach and pick up a day pass — your ticket to hop on and off all day across False Creek. The ferries stop at Granville Island, the Maritime Museum dock, Plaza of Nations, and more. A floating adventure!

🔗 False Creek Ferries
🏛️
Museum of Vancouver & Vanier Park
Late Morning
✨ Fun FactThe Museum of Vancouver is the largest civic museum in Canada! It sits in Vanier Park alongside the H.R. MacMillan Space Centre and the Vancouver Maritime Museum — a whole cluster of great experiences in one beautiful waterfront park.

Take the ferry to the Maritime Museum dock. Explore the Museum of Vancouver — Vancouver's history from Indigenous peoples through to the counterculture 1960s and beyond. Then wander through Hadden Park and beautiful Vanier Park right on the water, with stunning views across English Bay.

🔗 Museum of Vancouver
🛶
Ferry to Granville Island — Late Lunch & Grazing
Afternoon

Hop back on the False Creek Ferry to Granville Island for a leisurely late lunch or snack. The Public Market is perfect for grazing — fresh pastries, charcuterie, local cheese, samosas, and more. Browse the artisan studios and Net Loft shops.

🔗 Granville Island
🌆
Ferry to Plaza of Nations → Canada Place
Late Afternoon

Take the ferry to the Plaza of Nations stop, then make your way up to Canada Place along the waterfront — taking in the sights, the seawall, and Coal Harbour views as you go. A great lead-up to dinner and the evening activities.

🍜
Dinner Out — Three Great Options
Dinner
🍽️ Pick your mood tonight!

Phnom Penh — A Vancouver legend for over 40 years. Cambodian-Vietnamese fusion. The famous butter beef and crispy chicken wings are iconic. No reservations, arrive early. Rated 4.4/5 with 5,000+ reviews.

Kosoo Korean — Lively Korean chicken and BBQ on Robson Street. Fried chicken, kimchi jjigae, huge portions, very affordable. Fun, energetic vibe.

Pho in Love — A beloved Vancouver pho spot for a warm, comforting bowl of noodle soup. Great for a relaxed, flavourful evening.

🔗 Phnom Penh🔗 Kosoo Korean🔗 Pho in Love
✈️
FlyOver Canada — Evening Grand Finale
Evening
✨ Fun FactFlyOver Canada is a flight simulation ride where you're literally lifted off the ground in a hanging seat and "flown" over Canada's most spectacular landscapes — while wind blows in your face, mist sprays, and scents like pine forests and ocean salt fill the air. Pure magic.

The grand finale of the day! Located right at Canada Place, this immersive experience takes you soaring over the Rockies, Niagara Falls, the Prairies, Prince Edward Island, and the BC coast. Book tickets online to secure your time slot. Explore the waterfront a little after, then walk back to the Airbnb to close out an incredible day.

🔗 FlyOver Canada website
